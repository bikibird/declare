Passage Help

Type the text that you want translated into a Speako8 speech string in the Passage text box.

Spelling matters. Translation relies on the CMU pronouncing dictionary. All words must be in English. Contrations are usually ok. If a word is not found in the dictionary, it will be omitted from the speech string. If you need to translate a nonsense word or other word not in the dictionary, try breaking it apart into english words. For example, Speako8 should be entered as Speak oh eight You can also enter a word that rhymes and then adjust the phonetics in the next step.

Punctuation matters. Periods, commas, and question marks affect the prosidy mark up. End sentences with periods for fallling intonation and pause or a question mark for rising intonation and pause. Commas add a pause without changing the intonation.

Phonetics Help

The Phonetics text box contains a phonetic translation of the passage without prosody markup. Depending on your preferences, you may want to edit this data.

Each sound (phone) is represented by a 2 character phoneme. Phonemes (and punctuation) are separated by slashes. Alternate pronunciations are shown in square brackets and are not included in the final speech string. Move the brackets to change how a word will be pronounced.

Phonemes for vowels include a stress digit. A value of 1 indicates primary stress, 2 indicates secondary stress and 0 indicates an unstressed syllable.

Speech String Help

The Speech String text box contains a phonetic translation of the passage complete with prosody markup. Audition the speech string by clicking the Listen button.

This string is used as the argument to the Speako8 say function.

The underscore character indicates a pause at the start of a clause or a stop consonant.

The numbers (separated by slashes) preceding the phonemes indicate the duration, intonation, and intensity of the sounds (phones) associated with the phonemes. These may be edited or added to change the prosody of the passage.

ValueExplanation
1.nnIncrease duration by nn% of innate duration.
-1.nnDecrease duration by nn% of innate duration./td>
2.nnRaise volume by nn%.
-2.nnLower volume by nn%.
3.nnRaise fundamental voice pitch by nn% of intonation.
-3.nnlower fundamental voice pitch by nn% of intonation.
3Raise fundamental voice pitch by 100% of intonation.
-3Lower fundamental voice pitch by 100% of intonation
Voice Parameters Help

The Pico-8 console plays the speech string. The string is initially loaded with the Listen button. To repeat the speech, press ❎. If the console loses focus, click the silver frame.

Use the up and down arrows to navigate the voice options menu. Use the left and right arrows to alter the values. See the Speako8 documentation for information on setting voice options in your own cartridges.

OptionRangeExplanation
Pitch60-230Fundamental pitch of voice (F0) in hertz
Rate.1-2Standard rate of speech divisor— below 1 is slower; above 1 is faster.
Volume.1-2Standard volume factor— below 1 is quieter; above 1 is louder.
Quality.1-2Glottis open period— below .5 is creakier voice; .5 is modal voice; 1 is breathy voice; above 1 is weaker voice.

Bandwidth.5-5Factor by which to alter bandwidth of formant frequencies (F1, F2, F3)— below 1 narrows the bandwidth of the formants; above 1 widens. When formants are shifted upward, it is recommended to widen bandwidths as well.
Whisper1 or 2Speaking mode— Normal voice is 1; whispering is 2.
